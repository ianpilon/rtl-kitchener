What reefer repairs can be completed on-site versus in-shop?

Most electrical, control system, and minor mechanical repairs can be completed on-site, including control board swaps, sensor replacements, and refrigerant leak repairs. Major compressor or evaporator work typically requires shop facilities.

Our roadside emergency service recently handled a Carrier unit that lost cooling with frozen food cargo on Highway 7 near Guelph. The technician diagnosed a failed discharge air sensor, replaced it on-site, and restored proper cooling within 90 minutes. Common on-site repairs include thermistor replacements, control board diagnostics, refrigerant top-offs for R-404A and R-452A systems, and electrical troubleshooting.

Temperature drift on refrigerated trailers often traces to degraded thermistors, where our technicians test return-air and discharge-air sensors against OEM tolerance specifications and replace only components that fall outside acceptable ranges.

RTS technicians carry portable refrigerant recovery equipment, multimeters, and pressure testing tools to complete most repairs without towing. For newer A2L refrigerant systems using R-454C, our certified technicians follow enhanced safety protocols for roadside service.
